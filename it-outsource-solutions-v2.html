What is IT Outsourcing?

According to recent data from Spiceworks' Ziff Davis, because of Covid-19, businesses are changing their business plans:

  • 44% are increasing the pace of their digital transformation
  • 33% are tightening their security and risk governance management
  • 30% are developing additional training resources for remote workers
  • 36% are working on improving their IT operations and systems performance
  • 32% are planning to connect with employees through standardized, secure, and intuitive tools
  • 27% are polishing their current disaster recovery setup

Is your business looking to do any of the following listed above? Almost all organizations were forced to rethink their overall technology strategy and IT infrastructure after COVID-19. More workplaces are adopting a remote or hybrid work model, which means more equipment and better infrastructure is necessary to support workers in order for core business functions to get done. Many companies are also moving into digital transformation, which, according to the Enterprises Project, is defined as "...the integration of digital technology into all areas of a business, changing how you operate and deliver value to customers. It’s also a cultural change that requires organizations to continually challenge the status quo, experiment, and get comfortable with failure. Moving into digital transformation allows for a company to be more innovative."

Many industries have felt this pressure to really rethink the strategy behind their IT and innovation, including hospitals and health systems, retail, nonprofits, and consumer financial services. IT outsourcing allows for organizations to use third-party service to handle some part of its business functions, allowing its internal IT department, as well as other departments and functions, to focus on other priorities.

What are the benefits of IT outsourcing?

There are several benefits to outsourcing IT for your organization, including:

  • Cost savings
  • Access to global talent
  • Time savings
  • Ability to upscale quicker
  • Better workflow and productivity

Cost savings with IT outsourcing

Many companies were affected financially by the COVID-19 pandemic, and looking for IT outsourcing can help with cost savings and effectiveness. By choosing to opt for IT outsourcing instead of continually investing in internal IT department initiatives, companies can recuperate some of the money they may have lost because of the pandemic. There are several ways that IT outsourcing makes sense financially for organizations: by outsourcing your company’s IT needs, you will have less internal team members to pay – and not just salaries, but also employee medical insurance, travel allowance, and pension plans.

Choosing to invest in IT outsourcing also means less risk with talent acquisitions and retention – you don’t have to worry about team members leaving, especially after they’ve received training the company has paid for. By choosing to outsource, you only pay for the package you choose, and you don’t have to worry about investing in employee insurance, taxes, training, and other costs that can really add up.

Less workspace needed with IT outsourcing

Growing your internal IT team means having to invest in more space, equipment, and upgrading your IT infrastructure to help support all of your team members.
